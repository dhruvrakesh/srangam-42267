# Site Gazetteer: Stone, Song, and Sea

## Kupgal–Sanganakallu
**Location:** Karnataka, India  
**Coordinates:** 15.4°N, 76.6°E  
**Type:** Acoustic archaeology site (ringing rocks + petroglyphs)  
**Dating:** Neolithic to Iron Age (~3000 BCE – 500 BCE)  
**Lead Researchers:** Nicole Boivin, Ravi Korisettar  
**Key Findings:**
- 200+ ringing boulders with wear patterns
- Zebu cattle, anthropomorphs, nets in petroglyph corpus
- View-sheds align with pasture corridors
- Acoustic signaling for herd management (H)

## Daraki-Chattān
**Location:** Madhya Pradesh, India  
**Coordinates:** 22.9°N, 78.5°E  
**Type:** Cupule site (Lower Palaeolithic)  
**Dating:** 200,000+ years BP (OSL)  
**Lead Researchers:** Giriraj Kumar, Robert Bednarik  
**Key Findings:**
- 1,000+ hemispherical cupules pecked into rock
- Among oldest known rock art globally
- Replication experiments show percussor types (M)

## Edakkal
**Location:** Wayanad, Kerala, India  
**Coordinates:** 11.7°N, 76.2°E  
**Type:** Pass palimpsest (multi-phase petroglyphs)  
**Dating:** Neolithic → Kadamba script (~3000 BCE – 500 CE)  
**Key Findings:**
- Trade corridor with 5,000+ years of marking
- Primary/secondary/mediatory sign morphology
- "Jar cup" motif similar to Indus Valley (M)

## Nartiang
**Location:** Jaintia Hills, Meghalaya, India  
**Coordinates:** 25.3°N, 92.2°E  
**Type:** Megalithic garden (menhirs + dolmens + groves)  
**Dating:** 500+ menhirs (1500 CE – present, some older)  
**Key Features:**
- Tallest menhir: 8.5m
- Ki Moo Shynrang (male stones) / Ki Moo Kynthai (female with capstones)
- Law Kyntang sacred forest adjacency
- Springhead-grove-stone clustering (H)

## Hastināpura
**Location:** Uttar Pradesh, India  
**Coordinates:** 29.2°N, 78.0°E  
**Type:** PGW (Painted Grey Ware) flood stratum  
**Dating:** 9th-8th centuries BCE  
**Key Findings:**
- Silt-flood interruption of occupation layers
- Mahābhārata flood narrative correlation
- Text-stratum dialogue (correlation ≠ identity) (M/H)

## Mawphlang
**Location:** East Khasi Hills, Meghalaya, India  
**Coordinates:** 25.5°N, 91.7°E  
**Type:** Sacred grove (Law Kyntang)  
**Key Features:**
- 80+ hectares of protected forest
- Taboo rules: no fruit/deadwood removal
- Water retention for 10+ villages downstream
- Hybrid stewardship model (customary + state) (H)

## Sundarbans
**Location:** West Bengal/Bangladesh border  
**Coordinates:** 21.9°N, 89.2°E  
**Type:** Mangrove ritualscape (Bonbibi cult)  
**Key Features:**
- Fishing closures keyed to monsoon onset
- Pālā-gān folk dramas encode cyclone memory
- Mangrove-fringe shrines with storm-repair offerings (M/H)
