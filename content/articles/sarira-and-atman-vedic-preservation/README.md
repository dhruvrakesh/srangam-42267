## Article Structure

This scholarly article examines how Vedic texts survived millennia of oral transmission and manuscript culture through a dual preservation system:

1. **Śarīra (Body):** The Anukramaṇīs—comprehensive indices that catalog every Vedic mantra by:
   - Ṛṣi (Seer/lineage attribution)
   - Devatā (Deity/theological purpose)
   - Chandas (Meter/syllabic structure)

2. **Ātman (Soul):** Sāyaṇāchārya's 14th-century commentaries that preserved:
   - Semantic meaning and interpretation
   - Ritual context and application
   - Philosophical framework

### Major Sections

- **Introduction:** The metaphor of śarīra and ātman applied to textual preservation
- **Part I:** The Anukramaṇīs as holographic indices (4 Vedas)
- **Part II:** The Gāyatrī Mantra as exemplar of tripartite indexing
- **Part III:** Sāyaṇāchārya's exegetical methodology and Vijayanagara context
- **Part IV:** The technology of preservation vs. mystification
- **Conclusion:** Implications for understanding oral/manuscript continuity

## Key Terms & Concepts

- **Anukramaṇī** (अनुक्रमणी): Systematic index of Vedic mantras
- **Ṛṣi** (ऋषि): Seer/traditional lineage attribution
- **Devatā** (देवता): Deity/theological purpose
- **Chandas** (छन्द): Metrical structure/syllabic pattern
- **Bhāṣya** (भाष्य): Commentary/detailed exegesis
- **Śarīra** (शरीर): Body/physical form
- **Ātman** (आत्मन्): Soul/essential meaning
- **Yajña-kuṇḍa** (यज्ञकुण्ड): Sacrificial fire pit
