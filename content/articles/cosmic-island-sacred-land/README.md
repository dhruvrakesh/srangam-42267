## Overview

A scholarly exploration of how ancient Sanskrit literature evolved from cosmic mythology (Jambudwipa) to terrestrial geography (Bharatvarsha), demonstrating a continuous civilizational consciousness through close reading of Vedic and Puranic texts.

## Key Topics

1. **Puranic Cosmography**: The Sapta-Dvipa model and Jambudwipa as the central continent
2. **Etymology of Bharatvarsha**: Synthesis of Vedic tribal legacy and Puranic imperial narrative
3. **Geographical Knowledge**: Mountains, rivers, and peoples of ancient India
4. **Vedic Expansion**: From Sapta Sindhu to the wider subcontinent
5. **Scholarly Interpretations**: Modern academic perspectives on ancient geography
