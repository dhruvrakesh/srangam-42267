## [2.0.0] - 2025-10-08

### Added

#### New Part IV: Echoes in Time (~2,500 words)
- **Section 1:** "A Memory of Emigration in the Baudhayana Shrautasutra"
  - Detailed analysis of BSS 18.44 passage
  - Ayava (Ayu) eastward expansion into Kuru-Panchala and Kashi-Videha
  - Amavasava (Amavasu) westward migration to Gandhari, Parsu, and **Aratta**
  - Scholarly debate: Witzel vs. Caland/Elst/Lal interpretations
  - Connection to Mitanni kingdom location
  
- **Section 2:** "Central Asian Legacies: Massagetae, Jats, and Shiva"
  - Massagetae confederation (Eastern Iranian Saka tribes)
  - Queen Tomyris and defeat of Cyrus the Great
  - Folk etymology: "Massagetae" as "Great Jats" (acknowledged as linguistically unsupported)
  - Jat clans' traditional Saka ancestry claims
  - Shiva's non-Vedic origins and Rudra's peripheral Rigvedic status
  - Kushan Empire (1st-3rd century CE) and **Oesho deity** iconography
  - Penjikent, Tajikistan archaeological finds (5th-8th century Shiva/Parvati idols)
  - Hypothesis: Pre-Zoroastrian Asura-centric traditions surviving in Central Asia
  - Saka migrations reintroducing these traditions to India
  - Syncretism contributing to Puranic Shiva's complex character

#### New Part II Sections
- **"The Theological Neutrality of the Early Rigveda"**
  - Analysis of *Asura* as honorific in early Rigveda (applied to Varuna, Mitra, even Indra)
  - "Older gods" (Asuras) vs. "younger gods" (Devas) distinction
  - Rigveda preserving pre-demonization theological state
  - Evidence that sectarian bias emerged later

- **"The War of Gods and Men: Evidence for a Physical Conflict"**
  - King Vishtaspa's conversion efforts preserved in Avesta
  - Zarathustra's Gathas describing persecution and forced flight
  - Explicit identification of enemies as Daeva-followers
  - Hostile inversion of sacred terminology as war's ideological residue
  - Indra becoming arch-demon (*Iṇdra*) in Avestan texts
  - *Asura* semantic degradation from honorific to "demon" in post-Vedic India

#### Enhanced Bibliography (15 → 25+ citations)
- **New Section I:** Primary Texts & Oral Traditions
  - Baudhayana Shrautasutra (Willem Caland critical edition)

### Part IV Speculative Elements
1. **Massagetae-Jats Connection:**
   - Folk etymology "Great Jats" not linguistically supported
   - Traditional Jat clan claims require genetic validation
   - Archaeological correlation limited
   
2. **Shiva Central Asian Origins:**
   - Hypothesis, not established fact
   - Requires more comparative iconographic analysis
   - Kushan Oesho = Shiva identification debated
   
3. **Saka Religious Traditions:**
   - Pre-Zoroastrian Asura cults in Central Asia speculative
   - Syncretism mechanism unclear
   - Needs more textual and archaeological evidence
